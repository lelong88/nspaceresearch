| Compensation Arbitrage Risk | Risk of seller construct a dummy policy to take advantage of high compensation payment, leading to below-requirement persistency | Medium | High | **Performance Management:** • Regular review the compensation / financing schemes to ensure the schemes offered are fulfilled the requirements and monitor its' effectiveness, including production, persistency and payouts at agent /branch/territory level, in order to define quality control & risk mitigation plan • The local Line1B team will actively monitor for suspicious signals and conduct in-depth analyses when triggered • **Analytic tool:** Heatmap, Compensation Analysis, VaR dashboard and the Agency Line 1B tool – integrating existing data and indicators, refreshed monthly to support review and investigation. • Perform data analytics on bottom-tier locations with the lowest persistency, high compensation ratios, and other risk indicators, followed by deep-dive reviews: – Locations with the lowest persistency to identify potential misconduct signs. – Locations flagged by Heatmap for multiple abnormal signals QA enhanced program as Minotaur requirement: • The QA team will review new production, agent profiles, recruitment, and sales activities at the document level to detect fraud patterns and cross-unit/location connections, including links to sales managers. • Conduct onsite visits to high-risk locations to address issues promptly and implement corrective actions. • ASBD monthly report on cost to FYP ratio & persistency trend & deep-dive into the payout data on the VaR breakdown at both individual and team level is reviewed as part of monthly campaigns approval process including arbitrage risk assessment **Policy replacement:** When a new policy is issued and a previously existing policy on the same life insured is terminated within 6 months before or after the issuance of the new policy, FYC payable on the new policy will be netted off against the corresponding amounts paid by the company on the replaced policy. This rule applies to both agents and managers. A new policy is determined as replacement policy if the life insured is the same regardless of product type or serving agent. **Product design:** All our products are designed to have very low first year surrender value |
