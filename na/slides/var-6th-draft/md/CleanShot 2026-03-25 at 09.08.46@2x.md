## 2020 – 2022: Stable Period (VaR ~$1.8–2.0 mil):

- Mass agent recruitment ~50k-60k new recruits/year with tremendous leader (UM+) manpower (6k – 9k)
- Agency FYP ~$200+ mil/year
- High production offset high payouts

## 2022 – 2023: Spike to ~$3.8 mil, driven by:

- 2023 was the outlier year — VaR/FYP peaked at 4.0% when business volume collapsed to $72Mn (from $204 mil in 2022) due to many headwinds and crisis; while compensation structures and commission rate remained unchanged (avg. FYC ~32%)
- Deferred payments from 2022 peak production flowing into 2023

## 2024 – 2025: lowest VAR in 6 years with limited volatility since Q3'2024:

- Absolute VaR amount is stably low at $0.3-0.4 mil. This period is also with smaller business scale: FYP declined to ~$53 mil, reduced manpower CA by year-end only ¼ - 1/3 of prior years, #UM also decreased (~2k) and commission rate reduced to ~26% from H2'2024 (vs. ~32% previously).
- %VaR/agency FYP is similar to levels observed in 2020-2021. During both periods, total %FYP contributed by core agents and new agents remained high (~80%). In addition, P13 in 2025 was improved to >80%, compared with ~50% in prior years, bringing it back in line with P13~80% recorded in 2020–2021.
