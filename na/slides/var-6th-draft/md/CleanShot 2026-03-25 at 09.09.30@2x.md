## *2026 VaR threshold rationale*

- $3.0 mil trigger point is calibrated against the 2026 forecast **of $2.5 mil** under the new compensation scheme and new ILP product launch
- Propose to maintain trigger point at $3.0 mil (+20% VaR from $2.5 mil) to absorb:
    - **Calculation-gap cases** due to timing difference between production and payment period, as well as a calculation-gap exists in the VaR formula where Income includes earnings from the entire team hierarchy, but FYP only captures direct-team production (exclude UM+ in direct team).
    - This means a UM+ with a large indirect team or with many high-productive UM+ in direct report can show positive VAR even without any risk— because their income reflects a wider scope than the FYP offset.
    - This ratio is consistent with 2025 observation, in which $0.05 mil/$0.29 mil in VaR (~20%) resulted from calculation-gap cases.
- At this stage, it still early to assess the impact of new ILP26 product on sales mix, however based on initial experience in Mar and base on our forecast of VaR under the assumption of ~40% sales mix comes from new ILP26 product, we'd propose to keep the trigger point at $3.0mil USD.
- To continue ensuring consistent sales quality, in addition to the existing monthly multi-indicator screening conducted by Line1B, Line1B will also have additional measures to monitor for new ILP26 sales *(see slides 10–13) for details).*
