# Claw-back Enforcement Considerations

![Source](../images/CleanShot 2026-04-04 at 13.15.39@2x.png)

## **<u>Context</u>**

- Manulife Vietnam's existing claw-back enforcement capability is largely only suitable for cases where an agent continues produce income with us, and we are able to enforce deductions/withholding of such future stream of income.
- This has been sufficient thus far, since debts owed by agents to Manulife Vietnam are not material, often in the range of USD100-500 only.
- However, with the GTF Sign-on Bonus amounts, the debt owed can reach material amounts.
- Hence, it is imperative that our business puts in place an adequate claw-back enforcement SOP to cater for this scenario.

## **<u>Committed Actions by Manulife Vietnam ELT:</u>**

- ==To put in place a Claw-back Enforcement SOP within FY2023, with reference to Regional Agency Loan Playbook==
- ==Claw-back Enforcement SOP to be jointly owned by Distribution, Legal & Compliance, and Finance functions==
- ==The Claw-back Enforcement SOP must include reporting back to local Agency Risk Committee==
- ==Where appropriate, we should consider to engage an external debt collection agency==

## **<u>Mitigating Factors:</u>**

- Maximum total sign-on bonus budget of VND15b for FY2023 (max risk exposure).
- Actual bonus amount based on debt notification letter or capped at 1x of last 12 months income.
- Sign-on bonus is at minimum approved through CAO and CFO, providing a level of gate keeping.
- GTF-SM selection criteria includes proof of employment, income and sales track record etc, to mitigate fraudulent GTF-SM recruits.
- Claw-back mechanism will include claw-back from immediate recruiting manager, providing "skin-in-the-game" for recruiting leaders.
