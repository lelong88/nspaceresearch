***2026 VaR threshold rationale***

- $1.5 mil trigger point represents a proactive 50% tightening from the legacy $3.0Mn threshold, demonstrating tightening signal and strengthened risk controls.
- It is calibrated against the 2026 forecast ($1.27 mil) under the new compensation scheme and new ILP product launch, and supported by a structurally cleaner salesforce following 2024–2025 conduct reviews.
- Proposed $1.5 mil (+17% VaR from $1.27 mil) to absorb:
    - ➤ Calculation-gap cases due to timing difference between production and payment period, as well as a structural asymmetry exists in the VaR formula where Income includes earnings from the entire team hierarchy, but FYP only captures direct-team production (exclude UM+ in direct team).
    - ➤ This means a UM+ with a large indirect team or with many high-productive UM+ in direct report can show positive VAR even without any risk— because their income reflects a wider scope than the FYP offset.
    - ➤ This ratio is consistent with 2025 observation, in which $0.05 mil/$0.29 mil in VaR (~17%) resulted from calculation-gap cases.
- The comprehensive monthly monitoring framework with multi-indicator screening remains in place regardless of threshold level *(details in slide 7)*.
