## 1. CONTEXT & OBJECTIVES

### What This Document Covers
The 2026 VN Agency Compensation proposal — a comprehensive overhaul of how Manulife Vietnam pays its ~14,000 active agents, ~2,400 unit managers, and ~225 sales managers. The proposal was prepared by ASBD (Agency Strategy & Business Development) for regional approval.

### Current Challenges Being Addressed
1. UM+ compensation is not attractive enough — leaders prioritize personal sales over team development, resulting in low recruitment and weak manpower growth
2. SM compensation is dominated by fixed monthly allowances — flat bonus tiers reduce motivation beyond threshold levels
3. Persistency (P13) standards need strengthening to improve sales quality

### 2026 Change Objectives
1. Enhance UM compensation attractiveness; focus on Top Leaders (MBA Pro, ~200 people) to drive recruitment and new agent development
2. Make PSM+ fixed allowance more variable — replace tier-based modifiers with proportional-to-target modifiers to encourage continuous FYP achievement
3. Strengthen persistency (P13) modifiers across all levels

### 2026 Business Plan Targets
| # | Target |
|---|--------|
| 1 | +47% growth in recruitment → 9,600 new recruits |
| 2 | 15% new agents convert to Manulife Pro Silver |
| 3 | +30% growth in MDRT+ → 350 MDRT |
| 4 | +20% growth in newly promoted UM → ~400 newly promoted |
| 5 | +67% growth in MBA Pro → 250 MBA Pro |
| 6 | Avg 7% increase in productivity (APE/active) |
| 7 | Overall channel active ratio improve by 22% |

### Cost & Risk Summary
- Incremental cost: ~1.1% FYP, offset by lower campaign/benefits expenses to stay within allowable PREs of 58% FYP
- CAR (Compensation Arbitrage Risk) increases from 3.0% (average) to 10% (Top Tier) — driven by higher investment in ~200 MBA Pro leaders with strong track records
- Risk mitigated by regular monitoring, P13 controls, clawback mechanisms, and deferred payments

## 10. COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE

### Market Positioning
Manulife is positioned as strong on producer benefits but weaker on managerial benefits compared to competitors.

| Company | Key Strengths |
|---|---|
| **Manulife** | Strong new agent investment (Silver Fast Track); progressive agent production bonus (max 70% FYC); specific MDRT reward (max 100M); retention bonus across hierarchy; comparatively low UM+ override |
| **AIA** | Balanced producer vs. manager comp; minimal new agent investment but pays recruiters 20% FYC for 12 months; progressive agent bonus (max 60% FYC + 50% SYC); wider agent retention bonus range |
| **Dai-ichi** | Rich leader override with high validation; specific new leader bonus; moderate agent bonus (max 2M/month + 32% FYC); MDRT reward max 45M; renewal benefits 2.5% SYC for new agents |
| **Prudential** | Rich leader override; strong M0-M1 new agent activation focus; rich top producer bonus (max 80% FYC + 9M/month + 15% SYC); MDRT 50M + repeated MDRT 10-20% SYC/TYC |

### Strategic Direction
The 2026 proposal accelerates reward for managers with payout skewed towards Top UM+ (MBA Pro) to make the UM role more attractive — addressing the gap vs. competitors who offer richer managerial benefits.

## 12. RELATED PROGRAMS

### GTF (Growth Task Force) Scheme
External recruitment program for leaders from competitors. MAC leaders typically receive all 3 layers: SM compensation + GTF scheme + MAC scheme. Key GTF components:
- Sign-on bonus: up to 1.5B VND (SM/SSM), 2.5B (DRD/RD/SRD), 4.5B (AVP+)
- Cash advance: up to 80% of debts to prior insurer
- Guaranteed Minimum Income (Year 1)
- GTF Year-end bonus: 5% of over-achieved FYP
- 3-year clearance: 40% Y1, 30% Y2, 30% Y3

### MAC (Manulife Agency Center) Program
Satellite office program for SM+ leaders in underserved locations. Adds a third compensation layer on top of SM + GTF:
- Office setup subsidy: 300-600M VND
- Monthly office allowance: 8% FYP × P13 modifier × office quality modifier
- Guaranteed monthly subsidy: 20-80M/month for first 3 years
- Currently 17 MACs contracted, 9 operational, contributing ~0.6% of agency FYP

### Activity Requirements (Under IBL Compliance)
New activity requirements for UM and PSM compensation payout, subject to local CAO approval. Required under Insurance Business Law to demonstrate that servicing contract holders are actively performing their roles.
