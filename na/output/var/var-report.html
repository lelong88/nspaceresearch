Methodology

How VAR Is Calculated

VARagent = Œ£ Total Payout (L12M) − Œ£ Total FYP (L12M)

üìä Rolling 12 Months

Sum of payout per month over last 12 months. Reflects full sales cycle and mitigates impact of late payments post free-look period (21 days).

üë• Direct Team Scope

VAR Direct Team of UM = Œ£ payout L12M ‚àí Œ£ FYP of all FA direct reports & UM. Only counted at months where agent holds UM+ rank.

‚úÖ Agency Aggregation

Only UM+ with VAR > 0 are counted (negative-VAR teams are zeroed). Agency VAR = Œ£ all positive direct-team VARs.

Payout Includes

  • First Year Commission (FYC) + rider bonus
  • Agent compensation (monthly, quarterly, yearly bonuses)
  • UM+ compensation (overrides, activation, development)
  • Contests, campaigns, financing schemes, benefits
  • GTF subsidy payments

Exclusions

  • CSV first year (only ~0.4% of FYP due to high allocation charge)
  • PSM+ income ‚Äî monitored separately by local team on monthly basis
  • Tax support scheme, cash advance (loan), retirement funds

III Manulife9
Calibration Challenges

Why the Threshold Number Is Noisy

‚è±Ô∏è Deferred Payouts

Commissions paid on a lag (sometimes >1 year) mean this year's VAR partially reflects last year's sales. The payout schedule artificially lowers VAR in the current year but raises it the next. The number doesn't cleanly map to a single performance period.

[Ref: overview-claude.md ¬ßDeferred payouts]

üí∞ One-Off Retention Packages

A single $500K‚Äì$1M talent retention payment to a key agent can spike individual VAR without any fraud signal. These are known, approved expenses ‚Äî but they inflate aggregate VAR. VAR is designed for mass monitoring, not capturing these one-off decisions.

[Ref: overview-claude.md ¬ßOne-off retention]

üìà Revenue Growth Paradox

Strong revenue growth pushes VAR up because top-performers perform even better and new recruits come in large quantity. High VAR can reflect genuine commercial momentum, not just risk. The 2026 growth target of +35% FYP will mechanically increase VAR.

[Ref: overview-claude.md ¬ßRevenue growth paradox]

Formula Mismatch: Income vs. FYP Scope

A structural asymmetry exists in the VAR formula: Income includes earnings from the entire team hierarchy (overrides on indirect downlines), but FYP only captures direct-team production. This means a UM+ with a large indirect team can show positive VAR even when the broader team is profitable ‚Äî because their income reflects a wider scope than the FYP offset.

[Ref: var-notes.md ¬ßFormula mismatch; slides-var/IMG_20260209_174403.md ¬ßNote on income scope]

Rationale for $2.0M

  • Tighter than legacy: Reduces the trigger from $3.0M to $2.0M ‚Äî a 33% reduction demonstrating proactive risk tightening [Ref: var-report-feedback-on-draft.md ¬ßThreshold adjustment]
  • Sufficient buffer: 2026 forecast VAR is $1.29M ‚Äî the $2.0M threshold provides a 1.55√ó buffer, enough to absorb normal monthly fluctuations without false triggers [Ref: IMG_20260210_104837.md]
  • Compensation scheme impact absorbed: New scheme triples VAR from $0.3M to ~$1.0M on same production. Even with 35% FYP growth, forecast stays well below $2.0M [Ref: meeting-notes-10-feb-2026-2.md]
  • Cleaner salesforce: Conduct reviews and high-risk team removals in 2024‚Äì2025 structurally reduced risk exposure, justifying a lower threshold than the 2023 peak era [Ref: var-report-feedback-on-draft.md ¬ßRisk exposure]
  • 2023 peak was anomalous: The $3.5M peak was driven by deferred payments from 2022 production + one-off retention schemes ‚Äî conditions that no longer apply [Ref: var-report-feedback-on-draft.md ¬ßVAR 2023 peak]

Open Items

Outstanding Questions & Risk Considerations

Unresolved Questions from Analysis

1. GTF Unit Anomaly

GTF units with VAR > 0 increase 18% (105 ‚Üí 124) in 2026 forecast despite total units declining 22%. GTF compensation scheme is unchanged and FYP grows 35% ‚Äî logically VAR/FYP should decrease. Root cause under investigation: possible tier-jump effects or team-size changes.

[Ref: meeting-notes-10-feb-2026-1.md ¬ßAnomaly]

2. GTF Subsidy Abuse Risk

GTF subsidy has no claw-back mechanism and is calculated on net production (no free-look requirement). Submit-cancel-resubmit loops can generate recurring bonus without genuine sales. Should this risk be explicitly addressed in the proposal?

[Ref: var-notes.md ¬ßGTF subsidy risk]

3. MBA Pro Concentration

MBA Pro VAR surges 3.2√ó under new scheme. Is the increased exposure to top-performer compensation acceptable given the business growth objectives?

[Ref: meeting-notes-10-feb-2026-2.md]

Risk Mitigation Already in Place

  • Monthly bi-conduct screening: Compensation payout ratios cross-referenced with other risk indicators to identify suspicious cases [Ref: IMG_20260209_174129.md]
  • Payment suspension authority: High-risk cases can have compensation suspended pending investigation completion [Ref: IMG_20260209_174129.md]
  • Claw-back clauses: High-reward scheme agreements include claw-back provisions for violations [Ref: IMG_20260209_174131.md]
  • Campaign enrollment suspension: Violating agents/UMs can be blocked from big campaigns [Ref: IMG_20260209_174131.md]
  • Contract termination: Ultimate sanction for confirmed fraud cases [Ref: IMG_20260209_174131.md]

Recommended Next Steps

  • Complete deep-dive on 119 non-MBA GTF units to explain the anomalous increase
  • Quantify GTF subsidy contribution to total VAR exposure
  • Validate 30%+ FYP growth assumption underlying the forecast
  • Assess whether GTF subsidy claw-back mechanism should be introduced
