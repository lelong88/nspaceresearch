## 1. WHAT IS MAC

MAC (Manulife Agency Center) is a hybrid distribution model — small satellite sales offices set up by SM+ leaders in untapped/underserved locations distant from Manulife branch offices. The leader operates the office; Manulife partially subsidizes costs and retains brand oversight through signage ownership and quality checks.

It sits between two extremes:
- **Branch Model** (Manulife's current model): company owns and operates offices, hires staff, bears all fixed costs. Effective in major cities but expensive and unsustainable in smaller markets.
- **GA/Franchise Model** (competitors like Chubb, Prudential, FWD, Generali): leaders fully own and operate their offices, bear all costs, but receive higher commission rates (typically 16-20% of FYP for office operations). More entrepreneurial but higher risk for the leader.

MAC is the hybrid: leader operates the office, Manulife subsidizes ~13% FYP in the first 3 years (8% variable + guarantee top-up) and ~8% FYP from year 4 onwards. Manulife retains signage and brand control.

### Why MAC Exists
- Manulife has 43 Agency Offices in only 36 of 63 provinces
- 85% of new business comes from top 10 provinces — extreme geographic concentration
- Branch model is too expensive for smaller markets
- Competitors operate franchise models and dominate in provinces where Manulife has no presence
- Need to attract experienced leaders from competitors who are used to the franchise/entrepreneurial model

## 3. MAC COMPENSATION STRUCTURE (3 Layers)

A MAC leader receives three compensation layers:

### Layer 1: Standard SM Compensation (same as any SM)
- Monthly fixed allowance by rank (SM: 35M, SSM: 45M, DRD: 60M, etc.)
- Monthly production bonus (6-8% FYC based on target achievement)
- Quarterly production bonus (1-5% FYC based on 1mAA target)
- Retention bonus (75% direct + 25% indirect of monthly+quarterly bonuses, paid over 3 years)

### Layer 2: GTF Scheme (for external/inorganic recruits)
- GTF Year-end bonus: 5% of over-achieved FYP (capped at 12x monthly fixed allowance)
- GTF Cash advance: up to 80% of debts to prior insurer
- GTF Sign-on bonus: up to 1.5B VND for SM/SSM, 2.5B for DRD/RD/SRD, 4.5B for AVP+ (capped at 1x last 12 months income)
- Guaranteed Minimum Income (Year 1 only): income floor based on prior income and stretch target
- Sign-on clearance: 40% Y1, 30% Y2, 30% Y3 — must hit APE target + persistency ≥75%
- 100% clawback of uncleared amounts if leaving

### Layer 3: MAC Scheme (office-specific)
- **One-off setup subsidy**: 100% signage + office construction (advance 1/3 at contract signing, remainder after construction acceptance)
- **Monthly office allowance**: 8% × actual FYP × P13 modifier × office quality modifier
- **Guaranteed monthly subsidy** (first 3 years): top-up to guaranteed amount if 8% FYP is insufficient, conditional on 1mAA target + activity requirements (COP/CS confirmed by RH)
- **Catchup mechanism**: quarterly checkpoints — if cumulative 1mAA target met, receive lump-sum catchup
- **Recruiter bonus** (for the leader who recruited the MAC): 50M one-off + 3% of MAC FYP for 12 months (capped at 100M total)

## 9. COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE — GA MODEL

### How GA/Franchise Model Works (Competitors)
Competitors like Chubb Life, Prudential, FWD, Generali, Sun Life primarily use the GA (General Agency) model:
- Leader (called GAD/GA Director) independently operates their own office
- Leader bears 100% of office costs (rent, staff, utilities, equipment)
- In exchange, leader receives higher commission/override rates (typically 16-20% of FYP for office operations)
- Leader has more autonomy but also more financial risk
- No company signage — leader's own branding or co-branding
- Leader essentially runs a franchise

### Competitor Rank Equivalents
| Manulife | Chubb Life | Sun Life |
|---|---|---|
| VP | TVP (Territory VP) | — |
| SAVP | RSVP (Regional Senior VP) | — |
| AVP | RVP (Regional VP) | SASH (Sr Agency Sales Head) |
| SRD | RSD (Regional Sr Director) | ASH (Agency Sales Head) |
| RD | RD (Regional Director) | SRH (Sr Regional Head) |
| DRD | ZD (Zone Director) | RH (Regional Head) |
| SSM | SD (Senior Director) | SZH (Sr Zone Head) |
| SM | DAD (Director, Agency Dev) | ZH (Zone Head) |
| DM | DM (District Manager) | SUM (Sr Unit Manager) |
| UM | UM (Unit Manager) | UM/PUM |

### What Makes GA Attractive to Leaders
- Higher total compensation potential (16-20% FYP for operations vs. MAC's 8-13%)
- Full autonomy over office operations
- Can build personal brand
- Entrepreneurial freedom
- No company oversight on day-to-day operations

### What Makes GA Risky for Leaders
- Bear 100% of office costs — if production drops, still paying rent/staff
- No guaranteed minimum income
- No company safety net
- Higher personal financial exposure
- If they leave, lose everything (no signage, no brand)

## 10. MAC vs. GA — COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### MAC Advantages Over GA
1. **Financial safety net**: 3-year guaranteed minimum subsidy (20-80M/month depending on type)
2. **Lower personal risk**: Manulife subsidizes setup costs (300-600M) and ongoing operations
3. **Brand power**: Manulife signage and brand recognition
4. **Compliance support**: company oversight on quality, DC, P13
5. **Career path**: can still be promoted within Manulife hierarchy
6. **GTF benefits stack**: sign-on bonus + cash advance + guaranteed income on top of MAC scheme
7. **Hybrid model**: entrepreneurial feel with corporate safety net

### MAC Disadvantages vs. GA
1. **Lower variable upside**: 8% FYP vs. 16-20% FYP in GA model
2. **Company oversight**: office quality checks, P13 requirements, activity requirements
3. **Clawback risk**: 50-100% clawback if fail within 3 years
4. **Limited autonomy**: must follow Manulife design standards, location approval required
5. **MOC pressure**: quarterly performance validation
6. **Internal MAC restriction**: existing team production excluded for internal MACs
