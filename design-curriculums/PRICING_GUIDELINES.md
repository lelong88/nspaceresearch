# Curriculum Pricing Guidelines

## Currency

All prices are in **credits** (integer values). Credits are the in-app currency.

---

## Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Credits | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Free | 0 | Chapter 1 of any story series; one sampler per content type per language pair; onboarding/trial curriculums |
| Starter | 9 | Beginner mini-length (1 session) vocab-only curriculums — basic topics like greetings, food, directions |
| Light | 19 | Story chapters in a long series (≥10 chapters); niche professional speaking (nail salon, etc.); beginner short-length curriculums with 4 sessions |
| Standard | 49 | Everything else — the default price for a full curriculum |

---

## General Principles

### 1. Longer = More Expensive (within the same category)

A mini curriculum (1 session, ~10 min) should never cost the same as an extraLong curriculum (5 sessions, ~60 min). The length tag is the primary size signal:

- **mini** (1 session): 9 credits for beginner, 49 for intermediate+
- **short** (4–6 sessions): 19–49 credits depending on difficulty and series membership
- **medium** (4–6 sessions): 49 credits
- **long** (4–5 sessions): 49 credits
- **extraLong** (5 sessions): 49 credits

The jump from mini to short is where the biggest price gap lives. Once a curriculum has 4+ sessions with reading, writing, and speaking activities, it earns the standard 49-credit price.

### 2. Higher Difficulty = Higher Value

Difficulty reflects vocabulary sophistication, reading complexity, and writing demands. The pricing floor rises with difficulty:

| Difficulty | Minimum Price | Typical Price |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| beginner | 9 | 9–49 (depends on length) |
| preintermediate | 19 | 49 |
| intermediate | 19 | 49 |
| upperintermediate | 49 | 49 |
| advanced | 49 | 49 |

Beginner content is the only tier where sub-49 pricing is common, because the content is simpler and shorter. Once you hit preintermediate and above, the curriculum almost always justifies 49 credits.

### 3. Story Chapters in a Series Get a Discount

A user buying a 20-chapter novel is committing to the whole arc. Pricing each chapter at 49 credits makes the total cost (980 credits) prohibitive. Story series chapters should be priced to make the full series affordable:

- **Chapter 1**: Always **free** (0 credits) — this is the hook
- **Chapters 2+** in a long series (≥10 chapters): **19 credits** per chapter
- **Chapters 2+** in a short series (<10 chapters): **49 credits** per chapter (fewer chapters = less total commitment)
- **Standalone stories** (not part of a series): **49 credits**

Target total series cost:
- 10-chapter series: ~171 credits (0 + 9×19)
- 20-chapter series: ~361 credits (0 + 19×19)
- 25-chapter series: ~456 credits (0 + 24×19)

This keeps the per-chapter cost low while the total series cost still reflects the massive amount of content.

### 4. High-Value / Test-Prep / Professional Content = Standard or Above

Curriculums that teach vocabulary and skills directly applicable to:
- **Standardized tests** (IELTS, TOEFL, GRE, GMAT)
- **Academic disciplines** (economics, medicine, law, accounting)
- **Professional contexts** (business English, corporate communication, negotiations)

These should always be priced at **49 credits**, regardless of length. The value isn't just the content volume — it's the career/academic ROI for the learner.

### 5. Entertainment-Based Content = Standard Price

Movie, music, podcast, and song-based curriculums are engaging and high-effort to produce. They should be priced at **49 credits** regardless of difficulty level, because:
- The media tie-in adds perceived value
- Production quality (audio analysis, cultural context) is high
- They attract learners who wouldn't buy textbook-style content

### 6. Niche Professional Speaking = Light Tier

Highly specialized, narrow-audience curriculums (e.g., nail salon English, tour guide phrases) with short/medium length should be priced at **19 credits**. These serve a specific professional need but are narrower in scope than general curriculums.

### 7. Free Tier Strategy

Free curriculums serve as entry points. Every language pair and every content type should have at least one free curriculum:
- **1 free sampler** per content type per language pair (one free topic-based, one free song, one free movie, one free podcast, one free story chapter 1)
- **Chapter 1** of every story series is always free
- Free curriculums should be genuinely good — they're the sales pitch

## Decision Flowchart

```
Is it Chapter 1 of a story series?
  → YES → 0 credits (free)

Is it a beginner mini-length (1 session) vocab curriculum?
  → YES → 9 credits

Is it a chapter (2+) in a story series with ≥10 chapters?
  → YES → 19 credits

Is it niche professional speaking (nail salon, tour guide, etc.) with short length?
  → YES → 19 credits

Is it a beginner short-length curriculum with 4 sessions?
  → YES → 19 credits

Everything else (standard curriculum with 4+ sessions, any media-based, 
any academic/professional, any intermediate+ difficulty)?
  → 49 credits
```

## Anti-Patterns (Don't Do This)

- Don't price advanced content at 9 or 19 — it devalues the depth
- Don't price a 20-chapter series at 49/chapter — total cost becomes absurd
- Don't make all beginner content free — 9 credits is low enough to feel accessible while establishing that content has value
- Don't vary price by language pair — same content quality = same price regardless of en-en, en-vi, zh-vi, etc.
- Don't use prices other than 0, 9, 19, 49 — keep the tier system clean and predictable
