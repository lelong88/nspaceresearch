# Chinese-Language Talk Selections for vi-zh and en-zh Podcasts

These 4 Chinese-language talks are used as source material for both:
- **vi-zh podcast curriculums** (Vietnamese UI, Chinese target language)
- **en-zh podcast curriculums** (English UI, Chinese target language)

Both language pairs use the same 4 talks, differing only in user-facing language.

## Selection Criteria
- Delivered in Mandarin Chinese (not English)
- Available on YouTube with clear audio
- Suitable for HSK2-HSK3 vocabulary extraction (intermediate Chinese)
- Diverse topics and speakers
- TEDx/TED talks with substantial view counts

---

## Talk #0: 沒有「負面能量」是好事嗎？需要重新認識的「情緒反應」

- **Speaker**: 陳永儀 (May Chen)
- **English Title**: Acknowledge and Embrace Your Negative Emotions
- **YouTube URL**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uiJ4zibW8_M
- **Event**: TEDxTaipei
- **Duration**: ~15 minutes
- **Views**: ~3M
- **Topic**: Psychology professor May Chen challenges the common belief that negative emotions are bad. She argues that emotional reactions — including anger, sadness, and fear — are natural and necessary. Suppressing emotions is harmful; acknowledging and embracing them leads to healthier lives.
- **HSK2-HSK3 Suitability**: Rich in everyday emotion vocabulary (情绪 qíngxù, 负面 fùmiàn, 感觉 gǎnjué, 表达 biǎodá, 压力 yālì, 健康 jiànkāng), psychology terms accessible at intermediate level, and life/relationship vocabulary. The speaker uses clear, standard Mandarin with moderate pace.

## Talk #1: 留十八分鐘給自己

- **Speaker**: 蔣勳 (Chiang Hsun)
- **English Title**: Give Yourself Eighteen Minutes
- **YouTube URL**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6i7RcP39NB0
- **Event**: TEDxTaipei 2012
- **Duration**: ~25 minutes
- **Views**: ~2M
- **Topic**: Renowned Taiwanese writer and aesthetics scholar Chiang Hsun reflects on the importance of slowing down in modern life. He discusses art, beauty, and the value of giving yourself quiet time for self-reflection. He draws on examples from literature, painting, and daily life to argue that busyness robs us of our humanity.
- **HSK2-HSK3 Suitability**: Vocabulary about daily life and self-care (时间 shíjiān, 忙碌 mánglù, 安静 ānjìng, 美 měi, 生活 shēnghuó, 感受 gǎnshòu), art and culture terms, and reflective/philosophical vocabulary at an accessible level. Clear, measured Mandarin delivery.

## Talk #2: 不要太努力 (Don't Work Too Hard)

- **Speaker**: 曾之喬 (Joanne Tseng)
- **English Title**: Don't Work Too Hard
- **YouTube URL**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t7ZI9c6Ze7E
- **Event**: TEDxTaipeiFuhsingPrivateSchool
- **Duration**: ~21 minutes
- **Views**: ~1M
- **Topic**: Taiwanese actress and singer Joanne Tseng shares her personal journey in the entertainment industry. She discusses the pressure to constantly work hard, the toll it takes on mental health, and how she learned that sometimes not trying so hard leads to better outcomes. A talk about self-acceptance, career pressure, and finding balance.
- **HSK2-HSK3 Suitability**: Work and career vocabulary (努力 nǔlì, 工作 gōngzuò, 压力 yālì, 成功 chénggōng, 梦想 mèngxiǎng, 坚持 jiānchí), emotion and self-reflection terms, and everyday conversational Mandarin. Relatable topic for learners.

## Talk #3: 吃出更好的未來

- **Speaker**: 莊祖宜 (Tzui Chuang Mullinax)
- **English Title**: Eating for a Better Future
- **YouTube URL**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wcfLMfo6LQc
- **Event**: TED Shanghai 2013
- **Duration**: ~17 minutes
- **Views**: ~228K
- **Topic**: Food writer and chef Tzui Chuang discusses how our food choices impact the environment and our future. She argues that cooking and eating consciously — choosing sustainable ingredients, supporting local farmers, and understanding where food comes from — is both a personal pleasure and a social responsibility.
- **HSK2-HSK3 Suitability**: Food and cooking vocabulary (食物 shíwù, 做饭 zuòfàn, 健康 jiànkāng, 环境 huánjìng, 选择 xuǎnzé, 未来 wèilái), environment/sustainability terms, and daily life vocabulary. Practical topic with concrete, everyday vocabulary ideal for intermediate learners.
